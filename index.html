The math layer

Signals you can inspect.

Streak

S(t) = max(0, today - quit_date)

Days since the last use date anchors money, craving, and absorption timelines.

Elimination

A(t) = A₀ × 0.5^(t / half_life)

Half-life presets and custom values power the remaining amount estimate.

Absorption

input(t) = dose × min(1, t / window)

Inhalation and hold settings shape how quickly the amount reaches peak.

Day and timezone

Graphs line up with your clock.

  • Device-local time. Quit-day streaks use the calendar day on this computer.
  • Timezone-aware labels. Absorption usage time can be displayed as local time or UTC.
  • Manual nodes. Custom graph points are plotted relative to day or hour inputs you type.

Local-first by design

Your quit data belongs on your machine.

  • No required account. Demo values are stored with browser localStorage on this computer.
  • No remote database. Charts are generated in JavaScript in the browser.
  • Resettable. Wipe buttons remove the local demo data whenever you want.

Quikter is a local-first concept for people who want clear quit math without surrendering their data.
